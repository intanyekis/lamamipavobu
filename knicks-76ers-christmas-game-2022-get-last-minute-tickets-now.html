Forget jolly old Saint Nick — this year, celebrate Christmas with the Knicks.

Tom Thibodeau’s 17-13 team, currently sitting in sixth place in the Eastern Conference, will play division rival Philadelphia 76ers (17-12) on Dec. 25 at Madison Square Garden at 12 p.m. EST.

And best of all, last-minute tickets are available to see Julius Randle, Jalen Brunson and Mitchell Robinson face off against Joel Embiid, Tobias Harris and De’Anthony Melton.

At the time of publication, the lowest price we could find on tickets was $145 before fees on Vivid Seats.

When the two teams met in their first matchup of the season on Nov. 4 , the Knicks rallied in the fourth quarter and defeated the Embiid-less Sixers, 106-104.

“We were able to fight back and kind of just stick together,” point guard Jalen Brunson said of the improbable win. “We just found a way.”

Knicks 2022-23 schedule

With a little over 30 games under their belt, the Knicks clearly have a lot of basketball to play this 82-game season.

With that being said, you won’t want to miss when they play Eastern Conference leaders Milwaukee Bucks (Jan. 9), upstart Cleveland Cavaliers (Jan. 24), the competitive Brooklyn Nets (Feb. 13), the scary-good Denver Nuggets (March 18) and they close their season against the Indiana Pacers on April 9.

How to watch the Knicks on TV

Prefer to catch the game from the comfort of your own home?

We get it.

If that’s the case, this particular Christmas Day game can be seen live on ESPN.

You can catch all other Knicks games during the season on MSG Networks if you are in the New York area, or by using NBA League Pass when out of market.

Come Christmas day, there’s a lot more NBA to watch too.

The Lakers will play the Mavericks at 2:30 p.m. EST, the Celtics and Bucks tip off at 5 p.m. EST, the Grizzlies and Warriors go toe to toe at 8 p.m. EST and finally the Suns and Nuggets close Christmas at 10:30 p.m. EST.

New York Knicks news

Currently riding a seven-game win streak, the Knicks are one of the hottest teams in basketball at the moment.

While their offense has been a large part of the team’s continued success, so has center Mitchell Robinson’s defense.

“Every game, it’s protecting the rim, making good decisions, guarding the pick and roll,” coach Tom Thibodeau raved about Robinson’s play.
