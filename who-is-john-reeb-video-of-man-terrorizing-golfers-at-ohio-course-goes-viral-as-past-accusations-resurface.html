John Reeb has been arrested twice this summer alone for his aggressive behavior

This isn't the first time Reeb has gone into "hulk mode" on innocent strangers. He had previously been stopped by the police for alleged drug possession, hostile behavior, and trespassing, per .

According to local press sources, the 5 ft 9 inches man has already been detained twice this summer for acting aggressively outside of his home, including one instance where he threatened a neighbor while only wearing his underwear.

The reported that on July 26, Reeb allegedly harassed a guy named Christian William Griteman, 26, in his hometown while only wearing his underwear.

Reeb received a petty misdemeanor citation for unruly conduct under the Archbold municipal ordinance after police spoke with both individuals.

Three days later, at around 1.15 am, Reeb was reported to be following a car down the street while shirtless, prompting calls to the police.

Reeb fled to his house as police knocked, evading capture despite police tracking him down. He was eventually charged with threatening and impeding official business in relation to this event.

Additionally, on July 15, he was charged with disorderly conduct for reportedly shouting at Keith Perry, a 48-year-old resident of Archbold, as he was getting into his truck to leave for work early that morning.

According to , the police report claims that Reeb spat at the truck before attempting to spit on Perry through the open window.

Reeb was arrested twice more in August. On August 10, police made their most recent arrest of him after receiving calls about him reportedly "causing problems with others."

He had also been charged with an infraction warrant earlier in August.

John Reeb's other offenses include trespassing and possessing drugs

Ohio man Reeb's other alleged offenses include trespassing earlier this year and plotting an intentional riot in 2022.

He is also accused of speeding in a school zone in October 2007 and of possessing drugs in July and December 2015.

Reeb was also found guilty of driving while intoxicated in November 2017 and was sentenced to 150 hours of community service and a $560 fine.

Furthermore, Reeb was also the cause of a major car accident in 2020, which resulted in him being thrown out of his car.
