Early Years

His father began his career as a lecturer at Yale University before rising to become the university’s president and eventually becoming a representative of Major League Baseball. His mother is a stay-at-home mom who is also a certified English instructor who has acted in the past.

Age, height, and weight

Career

Paul Giamatti began his acting career in 1997, as Kenny “Pig Vomit” Rushton in Howard Stern’s film Private Parts.Stern has frequently praised the actor’s performance on his radio show, and he has been nominated for an Academy Award in the category of Best Supporting Actor.

During the year 1998, he also appeared in a number of supporting roles in big-budget films such as Saving Private Ryan, The Truman Show, and The Negotiator.After playing the major part in the film American Splendor in 2003, Giamatti began to garner critical acclaim. The next year, he rose to prominence as the star of Sideways, an independent romantic comedy.The actor won his second Golden Globe in the category of Best Actor in a Motion Picture – Musical or Comedy for his role in the 2010 film Barney’s Version.

Achievements & Awards

Paul Giamatti was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor in a Motion Picture for his performance in Cinderella Man.

Sideways, Private Parts, American Splendor, Thunderpants, Cold Souls, The Illusionist, Big Fat Liar, Barney’s Version, Straight Outta Compton, Love & Mercy, Win-Win, and San Andreas are just a few of the films in which the actor has appeared; he also has a series called Downton Abbey and a miniseries called John Adams.

Two Golden Globe Awards, one Primetime Emmy Award, and four Screen Actors Guild Awards have all gone to the actor.
